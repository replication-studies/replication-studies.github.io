About the Journal

The Journal of Fair Replication Studies (JFRS) is an Open-Access with a focus on replication studies in the social sciences. We aim to promote transparency, collaboration, and high standards in research by providing a platform for researchers to publish and review replication studies.

The word "fair" in our title refers to our commitment to fair pay for editors and reviewers, fair access to published papers, fair and transparent peer-review processes, and fair academic critiques in the studies published here.

Our ISBN number is 978-1-7380675-1-0 , and we are registered with Library and Archives Canada.

Why another journal?

We believe that there is a need for a dedicated journal that focuses exclusively on replication studies in the social sciences. By providing a platform for researchers to publish and review replication studies in the social sciences. The Journal of Open Source Software sets a good precedent for short form articles about software (1-2 pages), and here we aim to expand to long form articles that provide detailed findings and code (e.g., 5, 10 or more pages), similar to Papers with Code that has a focus on Machine Learning.

Does this journal provide a DOI?

Yes, all papers published in JFRS will be assigned a DOI (Digital Object Identifier) to ensure that they are citable and discoverable.

Is the journal indexed in any databases?

Not yet, but we are working on it. We aim to have JFRS indexed in major databases such as Scopus. The Journal of Open Source Software is not indexed either, nor is Papers with Code platform, but they are widely recognized.
